Modernismo

 

“Lo bello está formado de un elemento eterno, invariable, cuya cantidad es excesivamente difícil a determinar, y de un elemento relativo, circunstancial, que será, si se quiere, alternativa o conjuntamente, la época, la moda, la moral, la pasión”.

Charles Baudelaire

CUESTIONARIO PARA RESPONDER CON LAS PROPIAS REFLEXIONES

 

 

1.

1.1. ¿Qué quiere decir Baudelaire con que el papel del arte moderno es “destilar lo eterno de lo transitorio”?

 

1.2. ¿Cuál es el papel del artista en ese escenario moderno cambiante?

 

1.3. ¿Qué importancia concluyes que pueda tener el “tiempo” en el arte moderno?

 

 

 

2.

2.1. Why can we count the Renaissance as the beginning of modern thinking? But what the difference with modern art?

 

2.2. What role would the Enlightenment play in the shape of modern art concept?

 

2.3. In what way art and artists reflected, in 1800´s, freedom  and individuality as modern values?

 

3.

3.1. What do you think “art for art´s sake” means? Do you agree with this concept of art?

 

3.2. Read again Oscar Wilde´s extract from his essay. Relate it to one or some of the art theories and modernist values we’ve learn.

 

“A work of art is the unique result of a unique temperament. Its beauty comes from the fact that the author is what he is. It has nothing to do with the fact that other people want what they want. Indeed, the moment that an artist takes notice of what other people want, and tries to supply the demand, he ceases to be an artist, and becomes a dull or an amusing craftsman, an honest or dishonest tradesman. He has no further claim to be considered as an artist.”

 

3.3. Make a statement over the formalist art theory, from your point of view: do you think art should be judged only by its formal values?
